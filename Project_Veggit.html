VEGGIT

"Reinvent yourself"
The Challenge: Wellness Tools
Context

The challenge was to design for NWI, a fictional company which wanted to reinvent itself and develop tools to help people achieve their health and wellness goals. Even though NWI has numerous years of experience in the wellness field, their program had been slow to catch up with technology. They have seen a substantial drop in memberships and wanted to find a way to add value to their members. So the task to be completed was to:

  1. Create a digital wellness tool for their clients
  2. Update their image - create a new visual system that reflects their innovative and refreshed approach to wellness.
My Role

My role included all the design thinking process: from brainstorming for ideas to understanding the user, defining a final product, prototyping and testing it. This project was to be completed in 4 months, from June to September.

The UX Process

1. RESEARCH

With an initial idea in mind, an App especifically designed to help vegetarians achieve their fitness goals via a nutrition tracker, I did and initial background research to understand more about the target group's perceptions. The methodologies used where surveys and also 5 interviews. I believed interviews would help getting the full picture of the target.

Although the survey and interviews results showed the majority of vegetarians do like taking care of their health & fitness, the interviews uncovered a major concern regarding my idea: a nutrition tracking app may be good but it can turn into an obsessive behaviour and thus, affecting the simple experience of just being a vegetarian, adding the fact that these apps can be time consuming.

“I used to track some food choices but after some time it just takes up to much time to estimate portion sizes and add all items to the app”

“Good data, but time consuming to fill out”

“I feel like with an app I would give too much time to control my diet and it could turn into a little obsession”

Discovery

By putting these research notes into an affinity diagram I managed to identify potential pain points and opportunities.

Problem

Vegetarians like taking care of their health, however, nutrition tracking can be time consuming and obsessing.

Nevertheless, this insights helped me identifying what their real problem was: vegetarians that workout or exercise do not have enough time to plan their meals.

And motivated me to focus in another direction by addressing this problem with the following idea:

Final Solution

“An app for vegetarians to plan their meals based on the groceries they have purchased or that they have on their fridge”. No hassle, no overthinking, just delicious meals based on what they already have.

3. PROTOTYPE

Vegetarians like taking care of their health, however, nutrition tracking can be time consuming and obsessing, and in the end, they stop enjoying the fact of being just healthy. (add facts)

The real problem I identified was that: vegetarians that workout or exercise do not have enough time to plan what they eat and although it would be interesting to know exactly what they consume, they don’t want to obsess with their diet by keeping track of the quantity of nutrients they consume.

This helped me rethinking to a more suitable idea:

“An app for vegetarians to plan their meals based on the groceries they have purchased/on their fridge”. This would be a prettier option as it would be focusing on presenting delicious meals based on what they already have.
